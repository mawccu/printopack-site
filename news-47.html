PVC VS PET-G Shrink Films

← Back to news

Since PVC Shrink film contains Chlorine content, Europe and many countries have gradually banned the use of Chlorine-containing substances.

PET-G is also known as Copolymer Polyethylene Terephthalate Glycol. It’s a form of polyester that can be extruded or molded into almost any product.

PET-G has increased its share in the shrink sleeve market for many reasons, and most multinational brands are replacing  PVC with PET-G because of environmental concerns.

 

Here are some of the benefits when using PET-G:

  • Competitive price compared to PVC shrink sleeves.
  • Excellent Printability and smooth surface.
  •  Higher Shrinkage compared to PVC. Shrinkage up to 75%, suitable for narrow neck & full-body cover bottles.
  •  Low-temperature shrinkage so customers can improve their productivity.
  •  Environmentally safe. (being SVHC & RoHS complied)
  • High-Resolution Graphics, clarity, and High-quality prints.
  • PET-G shrink films are 100% recyclable.
  • Low smoke density and toxicity.
  • Low shrink grade 62% and high shrink grade 75% are available as per requirements.
  • Easy to mold and thermoform.
